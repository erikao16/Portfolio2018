Bella Castilla Redesign

As part of Adobe Education Exchange's "UX to UI" online course, I redesigned an interior design website.

bc-mac

In searching for a summer job, I stumbled upon the website for a local interior design and furniture store. It had an extensive number of usability issues. These included hard-to-read text and a lack of information architecture, on top of it not being responsive.

Persona1

Persona2

The first thing I did was create personas for customers of the store. They were based on my knowledge of the area's demographic, and knowledge that this was a higher-end store. In hindsight, I should've asked the store owner about his customer demographic instead of making assumptions.

Style Guide

I followed this with a style guide, deciding on a contemporary theme to give the website a more polished look. However, one of my classmates pointed out that the logo seemed more rustic and traditional, which conflicted with this theme. If I were to do this project again, I would change the theme to be consistent with this.

Site Map

Next came the site map. I had a sample user (my mom!) do a card-sorting activity to see whether the organization made sense. In doing so, I learned that the original categories weren't as confusing as I had assumed, and I revised the categories I had initially created.

Reflection

I missed an opportunity to talk to the store owner about these designs; by talking to him, I would've gained a better understanding of the users, as well as aligning my design with the store's brand. As this was more of a UI project, I didn't take the store's mission and values into consideration either; the focus of the project was more on reorganizing the website, and making aesthetic changes. Were I to take this on a second time, I would work directly with the store owner and a web developer to make sure I was staying on-brand.
